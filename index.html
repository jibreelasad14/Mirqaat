§ 01

Epistemology of Logic

مباحث العلم
Be ready to state the Māturīdī definition verbatim (الحالة الانجلائية) and explain why it preserves God's knowledge as eternal (قديم).

Māturīdī position (detailed): Knowledge is a simple attribute with relation (صفة بسيطة ذات إضافة), called the manifestational state (الحالة الانجلائية). The knower is described by it as by other attributes. It does not arise upon connection to the known — thereby preserving God's knowledge as eternal (قديم) without requiring objects of knowledge to be co-eternal.

Al-Ghazālī / al-Rāzī critique: Ḥuṣūl (attainment) is an abstracted secondary concept (انتزاعي), not the essence of knowledge itself.

Muḥammad Ilyās (الحق): Knowledge is an epistemological state (حالة إدراكية) reconciling both presence and correspondence.

Division of Knowledge: بديهي and كسبي

The division into تصور / تصديق applies only to العلم الحادث الحصولي (acquired, attained knowledge of created beings). This is the position of the majority of محققون.

Imam al-Rāzī's minority position: All taṣawwurāt are badīhī. Therefore only taṣdīqāt divide into badīhī and naẓarī.

TypeSignifierCauseExample

Logic studies only Type 1 (conventional verbal indication) because communication and learning occur most easily through it.

The Three Divisions of Conventional Verbal Indication

المطابقة — Correspondence
The word indicates the complete meaning for which it was placed, from the perspective of its being placed for that meaning. (من حيث إنه وضع له)
التضمن — Implication
The word indicates a part of its placed meaning (conceptual, not necessarily physical part).
الالتزام — Necessary Concomitance
The word indicates a meaning outside the placed meaning that is necessarily connected to it, either rationally (عقلي) or customarily (عرفي).

Conditions for الالتزام

The indicated meaning must be: (1) outside the subject matter, (2) necessarily connected, and the necessity may be mental (e.g., البصر for العمى) or customary (e.g., الجود for حاتم).

TermArabicDefinitionExample
Bayyin bi-al-maʻnā al-aʻammبين بالمعنى الأعمConceiving both terms yields certainty of necessityقابل العلم for إنسان
Bayyin bi-al-maʻnā al-akhassبين بالمعنى الأخصConceiving the subject forces conceiving the concomitantالبصر for العمى
Ghayr bayyinغير بينRequires proof; rare—
Al-Rāzī Objection: Every meaning has the concomitant "ليس غيره" (a thing is not other than itself). If this qualifies as التزام, no مطابقة can exist without التزام — making the rule "مطابقة can exist without التزام" false.

Response: "ليس غيره" is only bayyin bi-al-maʻnā al-aʻamm, not al-akhass. You can conceive a thing without actively conceiving "it is not other than itself."

Admission: The commentator acknowledges inconsistency — logicians use al-aʻamm in their examples (قابل العلم for إنسان) but claim al-akhass in theory. The practically correct position: التزام refers to al-bayyin bi-al-maʻnā al-aʻamm, and "ليس غيره" is excluded only for practical reasons (otherwise every word would have التزام).

#ExampleWhy it is مفرد

Why "أضرب" is مركب for Logicians

"أضرب" indicates two meanings — the subject/agent (الفاعل) via the initial letter, and the action (الحدث) via the root. Logicians focus on meaning, not word form. Grammarians consider it a single verb because they focus on the word.

The Isnād Principle: Logicians evaluate whether a proposition (isnād — attribution of one concept to another) is embedded within the cognitive content of a word. In "أضرب," the concealed pronoun أنتَ (you) forms an immediate subject-predicate relation in the mind: "you — strike." This hidden isnād is what makes it murakkab for logicians even though it appears as a single word-form.

Exception: "يضرب" without a context-specified subject is mufrad for logicians — the subject is entirely open (Allāh, Zaid, or anyone), so no definite attribution is formed in the mind and only one cognitive meaning results.

Three Formal Conditions of a Good Definition

1. المساواة (Equality) — Comprehensive and Exclusive

ViolationTypeExampleProblem
Too narrowأخصإنسان = حيوان كاتبExcludes non-writers
Too broadأعمإنسان = حيوان حساسIncludes all animals
Completely differentمباينإنسان = حيوان صاهلDefines horse, not human
Circularدوريالعلم = إدراك المعلومRequires knowing معلوم first

2. الوضوح (Clarity) — Clearer than the Defined

Violations: defining by an equally obscure term, a more obscure term, or by infinite regress.

3. الإيجاب (Affirmation) — Prefer Positive over Negative

When affirmation is possible, do not define by negation (e.g., "الحركة = عدم السكون" is improper when a positive definition exists).

Contradiction (التناقض) — Eight Conditions

Definition of التناقض
Two propositions differing in quality (one affirmative, one negative) and quantity (one universal, one particular) such that one's truth necessitates the other's falsity and vice versa.

The eight unities required for contradiction (unities 1-6 must be the same; unity 7 must differ in quality, unity 8 must differ in quantity):

#UnityArabicViolation example
1Subjectوحدة الموضوعComparing propositions about different subjects
2Predicateوحدة المحمولDifferent predicates
3Timeوحدة الزمانزيد قائم أمس / ليس بقائم اليوم
4Placeوحدة المكانزيد في الدار / ليس في السوق
5Relationوحدة الإضافةقائم بالقوة / ليس قائماً بالفعل
6Condition/Part/Wholeوحدة الشرطDifferent conditions apply
7Quality (must differ)اختلاف الكيفOne affirmative, one negative
8Quantity (must differ)اختلاف الكمOne universal, one particular
Al-Fārābī's simplification: All eight reduce to unity of relation (وحدة النسبة). If the ratio of predicate to subject is the same, all other unities follow automatically.
§ 07b

Hypothetical Propositions

القضايا الشرطية
ion supplements the matn and is essential for the full treatment of القياس الاستثنائي (exceptive syllogism) and compound syllogisms.

Categorical vs Hypothetical (حملية vs شرطية)

A categorical proposition (حملية) attributes a predicate to a subject directly: زيد قائم. A hypothetical proposition (شرطية) expresses a relation between two propositions (the antecedent مقدم and the consequent تالٍ).

Two Types of Hypothetical Propositions

1. Conditional (متصلة) — "If…then…"

Definition — القضية المتصلة
A proposition whose parts are linked by "if" (إن/لو/كلما), indicating that the truth of the consequent (التالي) follows the truth of the antecedent (المقدم).

Two types of connection:

TypeArabicDefinitionExample
Necessitation (لزومية)لزوميةThe consequent necessarily follows the antecedent by rational necessityإن كانت الشمس طالعة فالنهار موجود
Coincidental (اتفاقية)اتفاقيةThe two parts co-exist as a matter of fact, but one does not necessitate the otherإن كان الإنسان ناطقاً فالحمار ناهق

Quantifiers for متصلة:

2. Disjunctive (منفصلة) — "Either…or…"

Definition — القضية المنفصلة
A proposition whose parts are separated by "either…or" (إما…وإما), indicating that the parts cannot both obtain simultaneously or cannot both be absent (depending on type).

Syllogism

القياس
Know the valid moods for all four figures. Also memorise the rule: النتيجة تتبع أخس المقدمتين and its exceptions.
class="def-label">Definition of القياس
قول مؤلف من قضايا يلزم عنها لذاتها قول آخر بعد تسليم تلك القضايا
Composed speech from propositions from which another statement necessarily follows due to those very propositions themselves, after they are accepted.
"لذاتها" excludes قياس المساواة (syllogism of equality), which requires an external premise: "مساوي المساوي مساو لذلك الشيء."

The Four Figures

Figure 1 — الشكل الأول

Middle term: predicate in minor, subject in major
Conditions: minor must be affirmative; major must be universal

كل م ص (كبرى)
كل س م (صغرى)
∴ كل س ص

The "perfect figure" — production is immediate and intuitive. 4 valid moods: AA→A, AE→E, IA→I, IE→O

Figure 2 — الشكل الثاني

Middle term: predicate in both premises
Conditions: premises must differ in quality; major must be universal

كل م ص / لا شيء من م بص
كل م س / لا شيء من م بس
∴ لا شيء من س بص

Only produces negative conclusions. 4 valid moods: AE→E, EA→E, IE→O, OA→O

Figure 3 — الشكل الثالث

Middle term: subject in both premises
Conditions: minor must be affirmative; at least one premise universal

كل س ص / بعض س ص
كل س م / بعض س م
∴ بعض م ص

Only produces particular conclusions. 6 valid moods.

Figure 4 — الشكل الرابع

Middle term: subject in minor, predicate in major
Reversal of Figure 1 — weakest figure

كل م ص / لا شيء من م بص
كل س م / بعض س م
∴ بعض ص س (or negative)

Produces only particular or E conclusions. 8 valid moods.

Rule: النتيجة تتبع أخس المقدمتين

If premises are…Conclusion is…Exception
Both affirmativeAffirmative—
One negativeNegative—
Both universalUniversal (in Figures 1, 2)Figure 3: A+A gives I, not A
One particularParticularFigure 2: IE gives O (particular negative)

Reductio ad Absurdum (قياس الخلف)

Structure: Assume the negation of what you want to prove → show this leads to contradiction → therefore the negation is false → therefore the original claim is true.

Example: To prove A converts to I (كل إنسان حيوان → بعض حيوان إنسان):
Assume "لا شيء من الحيوان بإنسان" (no animal is human). Combined with the original, we get "لا شيء من الإنسان بإنسان" — a contradiction. Therefore the assumption is false; therefore "بعض حيوان إنسان."

Dialectic (الجدل) — Expanded

Dialectic uses مشهورات — propositions widely accepted by the majority, the scholars, or a specific authority. Unlike demonstration, it does not require premises to be actually true, only widely received.

Types of مشهورات

TypeArabicDefinition
Universally acceptedمشهورة عند الكلAccepted by every rational person regardless of training
Accepted by the learnedمشهورة عند الحكماءAccepted within a scholarly tradition
Accepted by a schoolمشهورة عند الفرقةAccepted within a particular group or school

Purpose of Studying Dialectic in Logic

Dialectic trains one to argue from premises the opponent accepts, to expose contradictions in their position. Unlike demonstration which produces knowledge in oneself, dialectic produces conviction in the other.

Important distinction: A dialectical argument can be valid in form yet fail to demonstrate truth, because its premises (مشهورات) are not verified. The ability to construct a valid dialectical argument does not guarantee the conclusion is true — it only guarantees the opponent is committed to it if they accept the premises.

Rhetoric (الخطابة) — Expanded

Rhetoric uses مقبولات — premises believed because they come from a trusted source (authority, tradition, common sense) rather than demonstration. Rhetoric aims to produce assent in a general audience without their following a full proof.

The enthymeme (قياس خطابي): A syllogism with a suppressed premise, relying on the audience to supply what is assumed. Example: "He had the motive [implied: and anyone with motive is suspicious], therefore he is the killer." The middle premise is left unstated because the audience accepts it without it being spoken.
